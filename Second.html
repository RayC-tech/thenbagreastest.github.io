The Greatest

Curry Beats Boston

The 2022 NBA Finals was a defining chapter in Steph Curry's legendary career. Facing off against the Boston Celtics, Curry delivered a masterclass in leadership, skill, and determination, earning his first Finals MVP award. This defined Curry as a clutch performer and leader in the playoffs. He was constantly criticized for having great teams and below-average performances in previous playoffs, but this appearance locked Curry as a top 10 player in history.

The Greatest Shooter in NBA History

Steph Curry has completely transformed the game of basketball and is undeniably a top 10 player. Starting as an injury-prone player to a 2-time MVP with 4 rings. He’s considered the “Greatest Shooter known to man.” If Curry shoots, it’s probably going in. He’s also redefining how point guards play, where hitting a 3 is now required to stay in the lead.

The Greatest Shot in NBA History

The 1997 NBA Finals was a classic. Led by Michael Jordan and Scottie Pippen, the Bulls battled Karl Malone and John Stockton. Game 5, famously known as the “Flu Game,” saw Jordan deliver a 38-point performance while visibly ill, leading his team to victory. In my opinion, this was the greatest shot in NBA history.

The Greatest Winner in NBA History

Michael Jordan is widely regarded as the greatest player ever. His nearly perfect finals record (6–0) set a new standard. Aspiring players now strive not just for titles, but for undefeated records in the finals — a new expectation largely shaped by Jordan’s dominance.

The Greatest Finals in NBA History

The 2016 Finals were iconic. The 73–9 Warriors, pre-KD, were considered unbeatable. Down 3–1, LeBron James led the Cavaliers to an unbelievable comeback. This was more than a win — it defined LeBron’s greatness by defeating the best regular-season team in NBA history.

The Greatest Player in NBA History

LeBron James is one of the most dominant and influential players ever. For me, he’s the greatest — not just because of stats, but because of his longevity and consistency. Averaging 27/7/7 for 20 years without major injury is just as impressive as Jordan’s perfect Finals record.
